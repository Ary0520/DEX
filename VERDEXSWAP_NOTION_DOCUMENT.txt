1.  WHAT IS VERDEXSWAP?
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

VerdexSwap is a fully decentralized exchange — a DEX — built from the
ground up with one core promise: liquidity providers should not lose
money just for showing up.

On every other decentralized exchange today, when you deposit your
tokens to earn trading fees, you face an invisible enemy called
Impermanent Loss (IL). The more the market moves, the more money
quietly disappears from your position — often wiping out weeks or
months of fee earnings without warning. Most platforms either ignore
this problem entirely or promise a solution they cannot actually
deliver when markets get rough.

VerdexSwap is different. It is the first DEX to combine a classic,
battle-tested automated market maker (AMM) with a live, funded
insurance shield that actually pays liquidity providers back when
impermanent loss occurs. Every swap, every trade, every token exchange
on VerdexSwap seeds a protection fund. That fund exists for one
reason: to put money back in the pockets of the people who keep the
protocol liquid.

VerdexSwap is not a whitepaper promise. It is a fully deployed,
on-chain system where every piece of logic — swaps, fee collection,
protection payouts, pricing — runs transparently in smart contracts
that anyone can verify.

2.  THE PROBLEM WITH DEFI TODAY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

To understand why VerdexSwap was built, you need to understand what
liquidity providers — the backbone of every DEX — actually experience
today.

───────────────────────────────────────────
  THE IMPERMANENT LOSS TRAP
───────────────────────────────────────────

Imagine you deposit $1,000 worth of two tokens into a liquidity pool
on a DEX. You earn trading fees. Sounds great.

Now imagine ETH goes up 2x while you are in the pool. Because of how
AMMs work, the pool automatically rebalances. You end up with less ETH
and more stable tokens. When you withdraw, your position is worth
less than it would have been if you had simply held your tokens in
your wallet and done nothing.

That difference is impermanent loss. It is not a bug — it is built
into how AMMs work. And it silently destroys LP returns, especially
during volatile markets.

Research has consistently shown that a large percentage of liquidity
providers on major DEXs actually earn negative returns when impermanent
loss is accounted for — meaning they would have been better off just
holding their tokens.

───────────────────────────────────────────
  THE SANDWICH ATTACK PROBLEM
───────────────────────────────────────────

When you make a trade on most DEXs, your transaction sits in a public
waiting room (the blockchain mempool) before it is processed. Bots
watch this waiting room 24/7. When they see a large trade coming, they
jump in front of it, push the price up, let your trade execute at the
worse price, then sell right after — pocketing the difference. This
is called a sandwich attack.

In March 2026, a single trader lost approximately $50 million to a
sandwich attack on a SushiSwap pool — the position dwindled to just
$36,000. This is the reality of trading on vanilla AMMs.

───────────────────────────────────────────
  ORACLE MANIPULATION
───────────────────────────────────────────

Many DeFi protocols rely on live spot prices to make decisions —
insurance payouts, lending collateral values, fee calculations. Spot
prices can be manipulated in a single block using large trades or
flash loans. Protocols that rely on them are vulnerable to attackers
who can distort prices temporarily, trigger favourable outcomes for
themselves, and walk away with profits at the expense of everyone else.

───────────────────────────────────────────
  FAKE IL PROTECTION
───────────────────────────────────────────

Several protocols have advertised impermanent loss protection.
In practice, they either:

  •  Printed their own token to "compensate" you, diluting every
     holder and creating a death spiral when markets fell.
  •  Froze withdrawals the moment conditions got bad, trapping
     users when they needed their money most.
  •  Allowed instant exits during crises, triggering bank runs
     that drained the protection fund in hours.

These are not edge cases.  These are what actually happened —
specifically to Bancor, the most high-profile attempt before
VerdexSwap.

3.  HOW VERDEXSWAP SOLVES IT — CORE ARCHITECTURE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

VerdexSwap is built from eight interconnected smart contracts, each
handling a specific responsibility. Together they form a complete
exchange ecosystem.

──────────────────────────────
  THE FACTORY
──────────────────────────────

The Factory is the master registry of the protocol. Any two tokens
can be paired together to create a new liquidity pool. The Factory
uses a mathematical trick called CREATE2 to deploy new pools at
deterministic, predictable addresses — meaning anyone can calculate
a pool's address before it even exists, without needing to query the
blockchain.

The Factory also automatically categorises every pool into a tier
(Stable, Blue Chip, or Volatile) based on the tokens involved. This
tier determines how much fee protection and IL coverage applies to
that pool — handled automatically, no configuration needed for most
standard pools.

──────────────────────────────
  THE PAIR (POOL)
──────────────────────────────

Each pair is a self-contained liquidity pool containing two tokens.
It uses the classic constant-product AMM formula — the same battle-
tested math that powers Uniswap — so every swap is mathematically
guaranteed to be fair. The pool:

  •  Holds the actual token reserves.
  •  Mints LP tokens (proof of your share) when you deposit.
  •  Burns LP tokens when you withdraw.
  •  Charges a 0.3% base swap fee that stays in the pool as
     earnings for liquidity providers.
  •  Continuously tracks cumulative prices for the TWAP oracle.
  •  Is protected by a reentrancy lock, meaning no malicious
     contract can call back into it mid-transaction to steal funds.
  •  Validates every swap against the invariant, ensuring the
     math can never be violated.

The Pair also supports a protocol fee mechanism (described in the
Protocol Fee section below) that can be toggled on or off by
governance.

──────────────────────────────
  THE ROUTER
──────────────────────────────

The Router is the main entry point for users. It orchestrates
every action — adding liquidity, removing liquidity, and swapping —
and connects all the other contracts together.

When you swap, the Router:
  1.  Reads the pool's tier to know the current fee split.
  2.  Deducts a small portion of your input (the "vault fee") and
      routes it directly to the IL Shield Vault as insurance funding.
  3.  Deducts a separate portion as treasury revenue.
  4.  Sends the rest to the pool for the actual swap.
  5.  Enforces your slippage tolerance — if the swap would give you
      less than you asked for, it reverts the entire transaction.

When you add liquidity, the Router:
  1.  Records your exact deposit value and timestamp in the
      Position Manager — this is your baseline for IL calculation.
  2.  Updates the vault's exposure tracker so it knows how much
      outstanding IL liability exists across the protocol.

When you remove liquidity, the Router:
  1.  Retrieves your original deposit value from the Position Manager.
  2.  Compares it against what your position is worth today.
  3.  If you have been in the pool for at least 7 days and your
      position is worth less than when you deposited, it automatically
      requests an IL payout on your behalf from the vault.
  4.  Pays your IL compensation directly in USDC, on top of your
      normal withdrawal.

This means IL protection is seamless — you do not need to file a
claim, use a separate interface, or take any extra steps.

4.  IL SHIELD — THE IMPERMANENT LOSS PROTECTION SYSTEM
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The IL Shield is the most innovative piece of VerdexSwap. It is a
fully on-chain insurance system that protects liquidity providers from
impermanent loss without printing tokens, without central custody, and
without the failure modes that destroyed Bancor.

──────────────────────────────
  HOW IT IS FUNDED
──────────────────────────────

Every single swap on VerdexSwap contributes a small percentage of the
input amount to the IL Shield Vault. This is not a promise — it
happens automatically, in the same transaction as the swap, through
code. No one can intercept it, redirect it, or skip it.

The fees accumulate in the vault as raw tokens (whatever token the
user was swapping). A FeeConverter then converts these into USDC so
the vault always holds a stable, predictable reserve.

──────────────────────────────
  HOW COVERAGE IS CALCULATED
──────────────────────────────

Coverage is not a flat number. VerdexSwap uses a mathematical curve
called the CoverageCurve to calculate exactly how much protection you
are entitled to when you withdraw.

The curve has three inputs:

  1.  HOW LONG YOU HAVE BEEN IN THE POOL
      Coverage grows the longer you stay. It starts at 0 when you
      first deposit and grows rapidly in the first 30 days, then
      gradually approaches its maximum over time. After about 8 months
      of continuous liquidity provision, you can receive near-maximum
      coverage. This rewards loyal, long-term LPs and discourages
      mercenary behaviour.

        7 days  → ~11% of your tier's ceiling covered
       30 days  → ~39% of your tier's ceiling covered
       60 days  → ~63% of your tier's ceiling covered
       90 days  → ~78% of your tier's ceiling covered
      120 days  → ~87% of your tier's ceiling covered
      180 days  → ~95% of your tier's ceiling covered
      240 days+ → ~98% of your tier's ceiling covered

  2.  HOW HEALTHY THE VAULT IS
      If the vault has more than 150% of all outstanding IL claims
      in reserve, you get full coverage. If the vault is under stress
      (reserve falls toward outstanding liabilities), coverage
      automatically scales down. The vault can never promise more than
      it can pay — this is hardcoded into the math.

  3.  YOUR POOL'S TIER CEILING
      Each tier has a maximum coverage cap (e.g., volatile pools cap
      at 75%, blue chip at 50%, stable at 15%). No matter how long
      you wait or how healthy the vault is, you can never receive more
      than the ceiling for your pool type.

──────────────────────────────
  SAFETY MECHANISMS
──────────────────────────────

  CIRCUIT BREAKER
  If the vault's payout utilisation exceeds 50%, all future coverage
  amounts are automatically halved. This is a mathematical speed
  bump — the vault slows payouts before it can be drained.

  PAUSE THRESHOLD
  If utilisation reaches 80%, payouts pause automatically. Your
  withdrawal still goes through — you get your tokens back — but the
  IL top-up is held until the vault recovers. You are never trapped.

  PER-USER CAP
  A single user can receive a maximum of 20% of their liquidity
  value in one payout. Prevents whale manipulation.

  PER-PAYOUT POOL CAP
  A single payout event cannot exceed 5% of the entire pool vault.
  Prevents catastrophic single-event drain.

──────────────────────────────
  IL VAULT STAKERS
──────────────────────────────

VerdexSwap's vault has a second class of participants beyond LPs:
stakers. Anyone can deposit USDC into a pool's vault as an "insurance
seller." Stakers:

  •  Earn a share of the swap fee revenue (50% of vault fees go
     directly to stakers).
  •  Provide additional capital backing that expands coverage capacity.
  •  Are exposed to loss only if payouts exceed the fee buffer —
     stakers are the last line of defence.

To prevent a bank run (the exact scenario that destroyed Bancor),
stakers face a 14-day cooldown on withdrawals. They must request an
unstake first, wait 14 days, then withdraw. This means panic cannot
drain the vault instantly during a market crisis. Stakers know this
before they enter — it is part of the deal.

──────────────────────────────
  POSITION MANAGER
──────────────────────────────

The Position Manager is the bookkeeper of the IL Shield. It tracks,
for every LP in every pool:

  •  How much liquidity they deposited.
  •  What the value of that liquidity was at the time of deposit
     (in USDC, using TWAP pricing).
  •  When they deposited (weighted average, so re-deposits are
     handled fairly).

Partial withdrawals are fully supported. If you added liquidity in
multiple batches, your position is tracked as a weighted average.
If you withdraw half, you get the IL compensation proportional to
that half. The math works for every scenario.

5.  THE THREE-TIER POOL SYSTEM
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Not all token pairs are the same. A USDC/USDT pool behaves completely
differently from an ETH/MEME pool. VerdexSwap automatically assigns
every pool to one of three tiers — Stable, Blue Chip, or Volatile —
based on the tokens it contains.

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  TIER          │  EXAMPLE PAIRS       │  IL COVERAGE CAP        │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  STABLE        │  USDC / USDT         │  Up to 15%              │
│  (both tokens  │  DAI / USDC          │  (IL is nearly zero     │
│   are stable)  │  USDT / DAI          │   on stable pairs)      │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  BLUE CHIP     │  ETH / USDC          │  Up to 50%              │
│  (major assets │  BTC / ETH           │  (moderate volatility   │
│   + stables)   │  ETH / BTC           │   — meaningful cover)   │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  VOLATILE      │  Any token pair      │  Up to 75%              │
│  (everything   │  not meeting above   │  (high risk — highest   │
│   else)        │  criteria            │   IL protection given)  │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

The Factory auto-detects the tier when a pool is created. The fee
split and IL coverage ceiling for each tier are stored on-chain and
applied automatically to every swap. The team can override a pool's
tier and update fee parameters at any time, giving the protocol
flexibility to respond to new assets and market conditions.

In practical terms:

  •  If you are a stable-coin LP, your IL risk is very low, so the
     vault takes a smaller fee from your swaps and offers modest
     cover. Fair trade.

  •  If you are providing liquidity for an emerging token that
     might swing 10x, the vault charges a higher fee per swap and
     offers you up to 75% IL coverage — real protection where
     you need it most.

6.  FEE STRUCTURE & PROTOCOL ECONOMICS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

VerdexSwap has a multi-layered fee structure designed to sustain all
participants: traders, LPs, insurance stakers, and the protocol itself.

──────────────────────────────
  SWAP FEE ANATOMY
──────────────────────────────

When a trader swaps Token A for Token B, their input amount is split:

  ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │  TOTAL INPUT (e.g. 1,000 USDC)                  │
  │                                                 │
  │  ├─ Vault fee  (goes to IL Shield Vault)         │
  │  │   Volatile pools: 0.15%  = 1.50 USDC         │
  │  │   Blue Chip pools: 0.10% = 1.00 USDC         │
  │  │   Stable pools:    0.03% = 0.30 USDC         │
  │  │                                              │
  │  ├─ Treasury fee (protocol revenue)             │
  │  │   Volatile pools: 0.10%  = 1.00 USDC         │
  │  │   Blue Chip pools: 0.05% = 0.50 USDC         │
  │  │   Stable pools:    0.02% = 0.20 USDC         │
  │  │                                              │
  │  └─ LP fee (stays in the pool, added to your    │
  │     earnings as a liquidity provider)           │
  │      All pools: 0.30% = 3.00 USDC               │
  └─────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

The trader sends only the latter two portions to the pool and the
vault simultaneously.  The LP gets the base 0.30% — exactly what
they get on Uniswap — plus they are now also protected by the vault
being seeded with every trade.

──────────────────────────────
  INSIDE THE VAULT FEES
──────────────────────────────

Of the fees that reach the IL Shield Vault:

  •  50% is distributed to USDC stakers (the insurance sellers)
     as yield, proportional to their share.
  •  50% is added to the USDC reserve that backs LP protection
     payouts.

This creates a real yield for stakers funded entirely by organic
trading activity — not token emissions, not inflation.

──────────────────────────────
  PROTOCOL FEE (ON/OFF SWITCH)
──────────────────────────────

In addition to the above fee structure, VerdexSwap inherits a protocol-
level fee switch from the Pair contract — a mechanism borrowed from
Uniswap's architecture. When this fee is active, a small percentage
of the LP fee is diverted to a designated protocol fee address instead
of staying entirely in the pool.

This fee is currently OFF and will remain off during the bootstrap
phase of the protocol. It will only be activated by governance once
the protocol reaches a meaningful level of Total Value Locked (TVL)
and sufficient trading volume — ensuring that LPs are not penalised
during the early growth period. The community will be informed well
in advance of any activation.

7.  PROTOCOL FEE & TVL BOOTSTRAP PHASE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

VerdexSwap is designed for long-term growth, and the team recognises
that the early months of a protocol are critical. Liquidity providers
who take the risk of being early adopters deserve to earn the most,
not give up a cut to the protocol from day one.

Our approach:

  ✅  PROTOCOL FEE IS OFF AT LAUNCH
      Every basis point of the LP fee stays with liquidity providers
      at launch. Early LPs capture the full 0.30% base fee per swap
      with zero protocol dilution.

  ✅  FEE IS TIED TO TVL & HEALTH MILESTONES
      The protocol fee will only be considered for activation once the
      protocol reaches meaningful TVL thresholds and demonstrates
      sustained trading volume. Specific milestones will be defined and
      made public before activation is ever discussed.

  ✅  GOVERNANCE ACTIVATION ONLY
      No single address can flip this switch silently. Any protocol fee
      activation goes through a transparent governance process with
      advance notice to the community.

  ✅  HARD CAP ON FEES
      Even when active, the fee configuration in the Factory limits
      total fees (vault + treasury + LP) to a maximum of 2% in all
      scenarios. This cap is enforced in code, preventing any abusive
      fee setting regardless of who controls the protocol.

This approach ensures VerdexSwap competes hard for early liquidity
by being the most LP-friendly option available — then grows into
sustainable protocol revenue only after earning the community's trust.

8.  THE TWAP ORACLE — MANIPULATION-RESISTANT PRICING
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Accurate, trustworthy prices are the foundation of every DeFi protocol.
Without them, insurance payouts can be gamed, lending ratios break
down, and attackers can extract value by briefly distorting spot prices.

VerdexSwap uses a Time-Weighted Average Price (TWAP) oracle — a
technology that calculates prices by averaging them over time, not
just reading the current spot price. This means:

  •  A hacker cannot flash-loan their way to a $50M IL claim.
     They would need to hold a manipulated price for 30+ minutes
     continuously, which costs more than any potential gain.

  •  Prices used for IL calculations reflect real market conditions,
     not temporary distortions.

VERDEXSWAP'S TWAP ORACLE — UNIQUE FEATURES:

  LAZY TWAP DESIGN (No Keeper Required)
  Traditional TWAP oracles need a "keeper" — a dedicated bot that
  calls the oracle every few minutes to keep prices fresh. If the
  keeper stops working, the whole protocol's pricing breaks.

  VerdexSwap's oracle is permissionless and self-maintaining. Anyone
  can update it. The FeeConverter — which runs to convert accumulated
  fees into USDC — automatically updates the oracle when it notices
  the price data is getting stale. Because FeeConverter callers earn
  a 0.1% bonus on each conversion (capped at 50 USDC), they are
  financially incentivised to run it regularly.

  This means the oracle maintains itself through normal user
  activity — no infrastructure to maintain, no keeper to pay.

  AUTOMATIC WINDOW GROWTH
  The TWAP window (the time period over which prices are averaged)
  grows automatically between updates:
    •  Minimum allowed window: 30 minutes
    •  Recommended update frequency: every 2 hours
    •  Maximum staleness tolerance: 8 hours

  The longer the window, the more manipulation-resistant the price.
  If no one updates for a while, the window just gets larger and
  the price becomes even harder to attack. The system gets more
  secure when it is idle, not less.

  DUAL GUARDRAILS
  Every TWAP read enforces both a minimum window (30 minutes —
  prevents short-term price manipulation from being usable) and a
  maximum staleness (8 hours — ensures prices are never dangerously
  outdated). If neither condition is met, the oracle rejects the
  read and forces fresh data.

9.  THE FEE CONVERTER — A SELF-SUSTAINING MACHINE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

When users swap Token A for Token B on VerdexSwap, a portion of Token
A accumulates in the IL Shield Vault as raw fee tokens. The vault
needs USDC to pay out IL claims. That means someone needs to convert
those raw tokens into USDC.

The FeeConverter handles this, and it does so in a way that is:

  PERMISSIONLESS — anyone can call it, not just the team.

  INCENTIVISED — callers earn 0.1% of the USDC output (capped at
  50 USDC per call), making it worth running as a regular activity.

  MANIPULATION-PROOF — every conversion enforces the TWAP price as
  a minimum. If the actual swap gives less than 99% of the TWAP-
  derived amount, the entire conversion is rejected. An attacker
  cannot manipulate the spot price to get a bad rate — the TWAP
  would not have moved enough for the manipulation to be profitable.

  GAS-SAFE — conversions below $10 equivalent in USDC value are
  rejected, preventing spam and dust attacks on the accounting.

  COOLDOWN-PROTECTED — the same token pair cannot be converted more
  than once per hour, preventing rapid repeated calls.

  SELF-HEALING — if the TWAP data is stale when a conversion is
  attempted, the FeeConverter automatically updates the oracle
  first, then retries. If the oracle is too fresh to update yet,
  the conversion fails gracefully and the tokens are returned to the
  vault safely — nothing is lost.

The result is a fully autonomous flywheel: swaps happen → fees
accumulate → anyone converts fees to USDC for a small reward → the
vault grows → more IL protection is available → the protocol is more
attractive to LPs → more swaps happen. No team intervention needed.

10.  SECURITY ARCHITECTURE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Security is not a feature bolted on after the fact. Every contract
in VerdexSwap was designed with security as the primary constraint.

  ─────────────────────────────────────────
  REENTRANCY PROTECTION — EVERYWHERE
  ─────────────────────────────────────────
  The single most common way DeFi protocols have been hacked is
  reentrancy — a malicious contract calling back into a target
  contract before its internal state is updated, creating a window
  to steal funds.

  VerdexSwap addresses this at multiple layers:

    •  The Pair contract has a custom reentrancy lock on every
       state-changing function (mint, burn, swap, skim).
    •  The Router uses OpenZeppelin's battle-tested ReentrancyGuard.
    •  The ILShieldVault uses ReentrancyGuard on all payouts and
       stake/unstake operations.
    •  The FeeConverter uses ReentrancyGuard on the conversion function.
    •  Every contract follows the Checks-Effects-Interactions (CEI)
       pattern — state is updated BEFORE any external token transfers.

  ─────────────────────────────────────────
  OVERFLOW PROTECTION
  ─────────────────────────────────────────
  All contracts use Solidity 0.8.x, which has built-in overflow and
  underflow protection. Additionally, the Pair contract explicitly
  checks that reserves never exceed the maximum value of a uint112,
  preventing any silent wrapping behaviour.

  ─────────────────────────────────────────
  SAFE TOKEN TRANSFERS
  ─────────────────────────────────────────
  Non-standard ERC20 tokens can return false on a transfer failure
  instead of reverting. VerdexSwap handles this correctly throughout:
  the Pair uses a custom safeTransfer that checks both the return
  value and whether the call succeeded. The Router and Vault use
  OpenZeppelin's SafeERC20 library.

  ─────────────────────────────────────────
  INPUT VALIDATION
  ─────────────────────────────────────────
  Every external function validates its inputs before touching state:
    •  Zero address checks on all address parameters.
    •  Zero amount checks on all value parameters.
    •  Pair existence checks before any interaction.
    •  Deadline / expiry checks on time-sensitive operations.
    •  Slippage checks to protect users from bad trades.
    •  Identical token checks so no one can create a pool of a token
       with itself.

  ─────────────────────────────────────────
  ACCESS CONTROL
  ─────────────────────────────────────────
  Admin functions are protected by explicit owner checks.
  Vault functions are protected by router-only checks.
  The FeeConverter can only pull raw fees via a dedicated interface
  that tracks, verifies, and deducts balances in the correct order.
  No function bypasses these access controls.

  ─────────────────────────────────────────
  INVARIANT ENFORCEMENT
  ─────────────────────────────────────────
  Every swap on the Pair is validated against the constant-product
  invariant before finalisation. If the swap would leave the pool in
  a state where k = x * y is lower than before the swap (after
  accounting for fees), the transaction reverts. This makes it
  mathematically impossible to drain a pool through a swap.

  ─────────────────────────────────────────
  FLASH-DEPOSIT PROTECTION
  ─────────────────────────────────────────
  The CoverageCurve enforces a minimum 7-day lock before any IL
  coverage accrues. This prevents someone from depositing a large
  amount of liquidity, immediately triggering a fake IL claim,
  collecting the payout, and withdrawing — a classic "flash deposit"
  attack. You must genuinely be in the pool for at least a week
  before you earn any protection.

11.  WHERE COMPETITORS FAILED — AND HOW WE EXCEL
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Every decision in VerdexSwap was informed by studying what went wrong

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
  BANCOR v3 — THE IL PROTECTION THAT FAILED WHEN IT MATTERED MOST
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

  WHAT BANCOR PROMISED:
  Bancor marketed 100% instant impermanent loss protection from the
  moment you deposited. LPs loved it. The protocol grew to billions
  in TVL on the back of this promise.

  WHAT ACTUALLY HAPPENED (June 2022):
  When the crypto market crashed and liquidity dried up, the cost of
  covering IL became enormous. Bancor's protection mechanism worked
  by printing BNT (its own native token) to compensate LPs. As more
  BNT entered circulation, BNT's price fell, which increased the cost
  of further compensation — a death spiral.

  Simultaneously, a "bank run" began: LPs rushed to withdraw while
  protection was still being offered. Bancor froze withdrawals and
  suspended IL protection, trapping users at the worst possible
  moment. Legal action followed. The protocol never recovered.

  ROOT CAUSE:
  •  The protection fund was backed by an inflationary token, not
     real money. When that token lost value, so did the protection.
  •  There was no cooldown on withdrawals — panic could drain the
     vault in hours.
  •  Coverage was 100% instantly — no time-based accrual to
     discourage short-term mercenary behaviour.
  •  No circuit breaker to slow payouts as the vault was stressed.

  HOW VERDEXSWAP DIFFERS:
  ✅  Protection is funded by real swap fees, converted to USDC.
      No token printing. No inflation. Real money backing real claims.
  ✅  14-day unstake cooldown prevents bank runs. Built into code,
      cannot be bypassed even by governance in an emergency.
  ✅  CoverageCurve means coverage accrues over time — no instant
      100% on day one. Discourages mercenary LPs, rewards real ones.
  ✅  Circuit breaker halves payouts at 50% utilisation, pauses at
      80% — automatic protection before the vault is ever at risk.
  ✅  Vault health multiplier: if the reserve falls below 150% of
      outstanding exposure, coverage scales down automatically.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
  UNISWAP v2 — THE GOLD STANDARD WITH ONE GIANT BLIND SPOT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

  WHAT UNISWAP V2 GOT RIGHT:
  Uniswap v2 is the most battle-tested AMM in history. The
  constant-product formula works. The LP token model works.
  The invariant check is mathematically sound. VerdexSwap's Pair
  contract is built on the same trusted foundation.

  WHERE UNISWAP V2 FALLS SHORT:
  •  Zero IL protection. LPs take the full hit of every market move.
  •  All pools use the same 0.30% fee regardless of volatility.
     A stable pool and a meme coin pool pay the same — unfair to
     one side regardless of which way you look at it.
  •  The protocol fee (when enabled) is a blunt instrument — same
     cut from every pool, no tier-awareness.
  •  TWAP oracle must be explicitly updated by external keepers.
     If keepers lag, the oracle goes stale.

  HOW VERDEXSWAP DIFFERS:
  ✅  Built on the same mathematical base for reliability and
      auditability — then adds a full IL protection layer on top.
  ✅  Three-tier fee system: fees match the risk profile of the
      pool. Stable pairs pay minimal insurance premiums; volatile
      pairs pay more and get more coverage.
  ✅  Self-maintaining lazy TWAP — no keeper required.
  ✅  Protocol fee is off at launch, protecting early LPs.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
  SUSHISWAP — GOVERNANCE CHAOS AND THE $3.3M ROUTER HACK
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

  WHAT HAPPENED:
  In April 2023, SushiSwap's RouteProcessor2 contract was exploited
  due to an input validation vulnerability. An attacker provided a
  crafted argument that caused the router to interact with a malicious
  contract. Over $3.3 million was stolen, affecting users across 14
  different networks.

  In 2024, SushiSwap also faced governance manipulation accusations,
  deletion of its governance forum, and alleged disregard for
  community snapshot votes. Its TVL collapsed from over $8 billion
  to under $102 million — a 98.7% decline.

  ROOT CAUSE:
  •  Input validation failures in routing logic.
  •  Governance centralisation and lack of transparency.
  •  No sustainable revenue model; relying on token emissions.

  HOW VERDEXSWAP DIFFERS:
  ✅  Every Router function validates every input — zero-address
      checks, pair existence checks, amount checks, path length checks.
  ✅  ReentrancyGuard on all Router functions.
  ✅  Fee-based revenue model, not token emission dependent.
  ✅  Contracts are minimal and purpose-built — less attack surface.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
  GENERAL AMM SANDWICH ATTACKS (MEV)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

  THE PROBLEM:
  MEV bots monitor pending transactions and sandwich large swaps,
  extracting value from traders at scale. One documented incident saw
  a trader lose $50 million to an MEV sandwich on SushiSwap — the
  position was reduced to $36,000. These attacks are systemic across
  all public mempool-based DEXs.

  VERDEXSWAP'S APPROACH:
  ✅  Strict slippage enforcement: the Router always checks that the
      output meets `amountOutMin` before the swap finalises. Any
      sandwich attempt that would push the output below the user's
      minimum tolerance causes the entire transaction to revert.
  ✅  Deadline enforcement: every transaction has an expiry. If a
      transaction is held in the mempool too long (e.g., a bot is
      waiting to insert before it), the transaction expires and reverts.
  ✅  TWAP-based pricing for all IL calculations ensures that even
      if a swap is sandwiched, the IL payout calculation is not
      based on the temporarily manipulated price.

  Note: Sandwich attacks are a network-level problem shared by all
  public mempool DEXs. VerdexSwap provides the contractual safeguards
  available within the AMM model. Future integration with private
  mempools or MEV-resistant RPC endpoints will be explored.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
  ORACLE MANIPULATION ATTACKS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

  THE PROBLEM:
  Many DeFi protocols use spot prices from their own pools as oracle
  feeds. Attackers use flash loans to temporarily distort these prices,
  trigger favourable conditions (e.g., fake IL to claim insurance),
  and profit at the protocol's expense — all in a single transaction.

  HOW VERDEXSWAP DIFFERS:
  ✅  All IL calculations use TWAP pricing, not spot pricing.
      A flash loan that changes the spot price for one block has zero
      effect on a TWAP price averaged over 30 minutes to 8 hours.
  ✅  The 7-day minimum lock before any IL can be claimed means
      that even if someone could manipulate a TWAP (which would require
      holding an incorrect price for days at enormous cost), they
      would need to plan 7+ days in advance.
  ✅  The FeeConverter enforces a 1% slippage cap vs TWAP for all
      fee conversions, protecting the vault from bad conversion rates
      even if a token's spot price is briefly manipulated.
